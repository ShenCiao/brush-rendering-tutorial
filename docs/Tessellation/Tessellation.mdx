There are several other methods to render a basic solid stroke (but not considering the variable width).
You can find them in several papers and online articles.

<details>
<summary>Paper and article list</summary>

  - [Polar Stroking: New Theory and Methods for Stroking Paths](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3386569.3392458) (Very Hard)
  - [Drawing Lines is Hard](https://mattdesl.svbtle.com/drawing-lines-is-hard)
  - Instanced Line Rendering: [part1](https://wwwtyro.net/2019/11/18/instanced-lines.html) | [part2](https://wwwtyro.net/2021/10/01/instanced-lines-part-2.html)
  - ... Tell me more tutorials in the [discussion](https://github.com/ShenCiao/brush-rendering-tutorial/discussions/2)

</details>

They may inspire some approaches to optimize the performance of the articulated line rendering in the future.
To learn about them, I would recommend starting with Rye Terrell's [Instanced Line Rendering](https://wwwtyro.net/2019/11/18/instanced-lines.html),
whose methods are simple enough to learn.
